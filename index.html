About Me

My mission is to bridge the gap between policy and practice, ensuring that long-term care systems are not just efficient, but equitable and profoundly human.

Dr. Darren Liu is a tenured Professor at West Virginia University's School of Public Health, where he leads research and education at the intersection of long-term care, health equity, and public health systems.

With over two decades of academic experience spanning four universities, Dr. Liu brings a global perspective—shaped by his roots in Taiwan and his research across the United States, East Asia, and beyond—to every classroom, publication, and policy conversation.

His work is driven by a commitment to person-centered care, reducing health disparities, and preparing the next generation of public health leaders through innovative, competency-based education.

Isms in Health Care Human Resources

Jones & Bartlett Learning

ISBN: 978-1-284-20180-2

This concise, practitioner-focused guide introduces the concepts of "output maximization" and "central tendencyism" as frameworks for recognizing unconscious workplace bias. Designed for health care HR professionals and students, it provides actionable tools for redesigning teams, improving organizational culture, and building genuinely inclusive workplaces in clinical and administrative settings.

The Challenges of Health Disparities

Jones & Bartlett Learning

ISBN: 978-1-284-15609-6

A groundbreaking contribution to health care administration literature, this book was among the first in its field to tackle health disparities head-on. It introduces the "disparities chain" framework — a systematic model linking social determinants, care delivery, and outcomes — to equip future leaders with both the language and the tools to drive policy innovation and equitable health systems reform.

Research Themes

Long-Term Care Policy & Innovation

Advances nursing home quality through person-centered care and leadership in post-acute and long-term care, with insights from Taiwan’s LTC 3.0 reforms to inform system-level innovation and aging in place.

Health Equity & Disparities

Systematic reviews and studies on cancer screening disparities, healthcare education, and the “disparities chain” framework now adopted in public health curricula nationwide.

Digital Health & Aging

Pioneering work on health information technology in nursing homes, online health tool use among older adults, and introducing AI literacy into graduate public health education.
